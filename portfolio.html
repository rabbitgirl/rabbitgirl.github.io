music

While I've been releasing music through bandcamp since 2011, a lot of it is either no longer available, or was released while I was still getting started and isn't really up to my current standard. With that in mind, I feel comfortable recommending the following releases to get an idea of what I can do:

  • Rabbit Girl - Places of Interest (2013): Fast drums and ambient music. My oldest release that I can say I'm seriously proud of.
  • Rabbit Girl - Velveteen EP (2015): The first part of a follow-up to Places of Interest, building on some of the same sounds and ideas.
  • 108 - GREEN SIGNAL (2014): A short and weird funk/jazz/fusion album.
  • Cord Theory - Glass Colossus (2014): Droning, improvisational ambient EP, recorded using only a guitar, screwdriver, and loads of digital processing.
  • PARTICLE EFFECT - OVERFLOW PARADISE (2014): Very loud and very fast dance music. May not actually be danceable.

games

In addition to a number of more polished, long-term works in progress, I've made several prototypes and smaller games over the last year. Sources are available for some digital games upon request. All binaries are compiled for OS X unless stated otherwise; please keep in mind that they're also mostly unstable prototypes, so be careful.

  • Gone Home the RPG (analog prototype, October - November 2014): Developed for my Rapid Idea Prototyping for Games course. A pen-and-paper adaptation of FullBright's 2013 game Gone Home; focuses on guided player improvisation, roleplaying, and puzzle solving. [design document/pictures]
  • Recycle (digital prototype, November - December 2014): Developed for my Rapid Idea Prototyping for Games course. An arcadey hybrid card game/military strategy game that never really got any further than the first few rounds of digital prototyping. [design document/pictures] [binaries]
  • thomas0.5 (digital, November - December 2014): Developed for my Computer Graphics and Programming in C++ courses. A short, text-heavy adventure game featuring early versions of my original characters for Roots. Highly non-canon and very rushed, but finished, and playable from beginning to end. [design document/pictures] [binaries]
  • telefrag (digital prototype, March - April 2015): Developed for my Foundations of Game Design course. A simple arena deathmatch game focused on melee combat, short-range teleportation, and dying in walls. May be developed into a full game in the future. [design document] [trailer] [binaries]
  • Throw Your Snow (digital, January - April 2015): Developed with Jordan Dewitt, Korbin Shuffleton, Theo Tspakos, and Thiago Mendes for my Game Programming course. A physics-based game making use of the Leap Motion, focused on knocking falling snowflakes into the opposing player's yard. Requires a Leap Motion controller. Winner of the Most Innovative Game award at the Northeastern Annual Games Showcase 2015. [summary/pictures]
  • Project Mayura (digital, September - December 2015): Developed with Bruno Agnellini, Ryan Stewart, Tom Katzman, Zack Hickman, and Dean Thurston for my Game Concept Development and Production course. A 3D platformer concept and prototype, for which I contributed concept art, level design, sound design, and music. [summary/pictures]
  • Sclera (August 2016 - present): In progress 2D game engine. Highly non-functional. More to come. [project page]

code

I don't frequently code unless I'm working on a game, but I still take on small projects every so often. These are more like toys or little utilities than anything else.

  • suan-dl (November 2016): A small, cross-platform python script (and standalone executable) that rips mixtapes from the suan.fm mixtape creation service.
  • POKEMON AESTHTIQUE (December 2016): A little html/javascript tool for making Super Aesthetic pokemon teams. I wrote this because everyone else was doing it by hand, and I was too lazy to do that.
