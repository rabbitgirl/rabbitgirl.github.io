in the northern hemisphere of a
temperate, life bearing planet

lies a smallish town
called Linford

((pop. 7,124)
(est. 1025))

comprised
(mostly)
of

farms, residential
neighborhoods, a
small downtown,
a waterfront,
a small train
station

and so on and
so forth.

summers are short.
winters are long.
(and cold.)

the City is close.
(but not too close.)

life in Linford is quiet.
(but not too quiet.)

the woods are safe.
(but not too safe.)

this particular day
(June 28th, 1262)
is a cause for
celebration.

(for many people.)

summer is around the corner,
and for the denizens of
the Linford Regional
High School,

(and for another,)

freedom

is almost at hand.

the end-of-day bell

(a tone, actually;
sweet digital
herald of
escape)

is minutes away.

the end of the school year.
the end of the semester.
(and for a few of them:
the end of everything
that defines their
day-to-day lives.)


they can hardly wait.
